Focus wheel

"A belief is just a thought you keep thinking." — Abraham-Hicks

How this works

A focus wheel is a way of climbing out of a stuck thought one rung at a time, never the whole ladder at once.

Start with the stuck thing, plain words, the way it actually is right now. Acknowledge it once, clearly, without arguing with it.

Each slice after that uses the same small bridge: I am thinking about [the stuck thing], but I am also thinking about ___. The second half only has to feel a little lighter than the first, not true yet, not solved, just less heavy. Maybe it's the food in the cupboard, or the people who'd show up if you called, or the one window in the house that gets good light. Whatever's actually there.

Keep climbing slice by slice. You'll know you've found the right rung when something shifts in the body, a sigh, a loosening in the chest, an actual okay-ness rather than a performed one. That's relief, not positivity. Let it settle before the next slice.

When the eighth slice is full, the center asks who you are to live this freedom. That's the gridding, write the feeling in the body, not the idea of it, and let it land.
